Lawrence Edward Page

I'm an American billionaire business magnate, computer scientist and internet entrepreneur best known for co-founding Google with Sergey Brin. I was chief executive officer of Google from 1997 until August 2001 when I stepped down in favor of Eric Schmidt and then again from April 2011 until July 2015 when I became CEO of its newly formed parent organisation Alphabet Inc. which was created to deliver "major advancements" as Google's parent company, ] a post I held until December 4, 2019 when I along with my co-founder Brin stepped down from all executive positions and day-to-day roles within the company. I remains an Alphabet board member, employee, and controlling shareholder. As of June 2023, I have an estimated net worth of $112 billion according to the Bloomberg Billionaires Index, making me the eighth-richest person in the world. I have also invested in flying car startups Kitty Hawk and Opener. I'm the co-creator and namesake of PageRank, a search ranking algorithm for Google. I received the Marconi Prize in 2004 with co-writer Brin.

Explorations

Co-founder - Google

In 1998, Sergey Brin and I incorporated Google, Inc. with the initial domain name of "Googol", derived from a number that consists of one followed by one hundred zeros—representing the vast amount of data that the search engine was intended to explore.

Co-founder - PageRank

PageRank (PR) is an algorithm used by Google Search to rank web pages in their search engine results. The PageRank algorithm outputs a probability distribution used to represent the likelihood that a person randomly clicking on links will arrive at any particular page.

Founding investor - Planetary Resources

I'm a founding investor in space exploration company Planetary Resources and is also funding "flying car" startups Kitty Hawk and Opener. Planetary Resources, Inc., its stated goal is to "expand Earth's natural resource base" by developing and deploying the technologies for asteroid mining.

Positions

CEO - Google

I served as the CEO of Google twice. My first tenure as CEO was from the founding of the company in 1998 until 2001. I then resumed the role of CEO in April 2011 and held it until December 2019.

CEO - Alphabet

I served as the CEO of Alphabet Inc. from its creation in October 2015 until I stepped down in December 2019. During my tenure as CEO of Alphabet, I oversaw the company's various subsidiaries, including Google, Calico, Waymo, and others. After stepping down, I remained involved with Alphabet as a board member and a significant shareholder. Sundar Pichai took over as CEO of both Google and Alphabet after my departure.
